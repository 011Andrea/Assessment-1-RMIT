Project

As a sideline to a Cyber Security position, I would like to create a program to educate young people how to be safe it various situations and assess if children are being exposed to abuse. The purpose of the game would be to expose children various scenarios and get them thinking about their choices in real life. It would ask questions in a careful manner. The idea would be to expose and educate a child about many negative things that a young person can be exposed to that they may have never thought about. The game would offer real life solutions and could be used as a diagnosis tool for schools and child protection agencies. The focus would be on prompting the user to think about situations they may encounter throughout their younger years so that if the situations arises in real life, they have given some consideration to the consequences of their choices. It would offer them resources to talk about and report issues.



My motivation for this application is to prevent children from becoming the victims of crime, making choices that can bring a major negative effect to their lives or get them into legal trouble. I have a passion to bring this kind of education into the mainstream. Although these topics are something that people do not like to think about, they exist in society with 4 out of 10 children being subjected to abuse and neglect and 90% of these reports are of a serious nature. https://www.abc.net.au/news/2018-09-16/australia-facing-an-epidemic-of-child-abuse-and-neglect/10233898



The application could be used to discreetly assess if a child is being subjected to any abuse without intimidating questioning. It would educate the child of personal health care and safety. The program would give children advice on things to do to better care for themselves and others, giving suggestions how to deal with different situations. The program would include phone numbers like Kids Helpline and advice to inform a teacher or safe adult. The game would allow the user to the understand the consequences of their choices educating the player about real life situations along the way. For example, if they said that they would enter their personal information into a website. They could have the money they have earnt in the game stolen. This would inform the user of the risks of disclosing personal information online. It could include health care questions and education. The program could output funny cartoons that show the result, an example would be maybe dental hygiene. It would question if the child brushes their teeth every day. If the user said no a funny face with no teeth could appear. The child could give him back his teeth by interactive actions like brushing teeth and filling holes at the dentist. The program would address less serious issues. In amongst these questions and options the program could discreetly assess serious situations that are of concern making the assessment process less confronting. The game would allow parents or teachers to set age levels and the game would output storylines that would be appropriate for the age of the user. The game could be further developed for teenagers. When the game is set for this age it could assess and educate young people using more complex games and information. It could offer them scenarios with options to use drugs, commit crimes or practice unsafe sex. It could them make them aware of the consequences with humorous advice. It could offer them options could lead them into situations where they have to work out how to escape or save themself. It could include battles with the bad guys and working out the best way to get out of circumstances their choices have gotten them into. They could collect items along the way earning money as points, earning rewards for good choices and losing money and rewards for bad choices. The program would include carefully chosen comments to build the users confidence and self-esteem. At the end of the game, the program could output a report that can be interprated by a parent, teacher or child welfare agency. The program could then suggest if further action is necessary and suggest the appropriate authority or action to deal with the outcome.
